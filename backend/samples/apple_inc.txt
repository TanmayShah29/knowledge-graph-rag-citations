Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. It was founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne on April 1, 1976. The company designs, develops, and sells consumer electronics, computer software, and online services. Apple's first product was the Apple I, a computer designed and hand-built by Steve Wozniak. Tim Cook has served as the CEO of Apple since August 24, 2011, succeeding Steve Jobs who resigned due to health issues. Steve Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011. Before founding Apple, Steve Jobs worked at Atari as a technician. Apple's major products include the iPhone, iPad, Mac, Apple Watch, and Apple TV. The company also operates the iTunes Store, App Store, and Apple Music streaming service. In 2023, Apple became the first company to reach a $3 trillion market capitalization.
