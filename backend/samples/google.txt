Google LLC is an American multinational technology company focusing on artificial intelligence, search engine technology, online advertising, cloud computing, computer software, quantum computing, e-commerce, and consumer electronics. It was founded on September 4, 1998, by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were Ph.D. students at Stanford University in California. They had met at Stanford in 1995 when Brin was assigned to show Page around campus as a new student. Google's search engine quickly became the dominant search tool worldwide, handling over 8.5 billion searches per day. Sundar Pichai has been the CEO of Google and its parent company Alphabet Inc. since 2015 and 2019 respectively. Before becoming CEO, Pichai led the development of Google Chrome and Google Drive. Larry Page stepped down as CEO of Alphabet in December 2019, and Sergey Brin also stepped down as president. Google's major products include Google Search, Gmail, Google Maps, Android operating system, YouTube, Google Cloud Platform, and the Pixel line of hardware. Android was originally developed by Android Inc., which Google acquired in 2005.
