Tesla, Inc. is an American multinational automotive and clean energy company headquartered in Austin, Texas. It was incorporated in 2003 by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning. Elon Musk joined the company in 2004 as chairman of the board after leading Tesla's Series A funding round. JB Straubel, a Stanford graduate and battery expert, also joined early and became CTO. Tesla's first vehicle, the Roadster, was launched in 2008. The company went public in 2010 with an IPO that raised $226 million. Elon Musk became CEO of Tesla in 2008, succeeding Ze'ev Drori. Before Tesla, Elon Musk co-founded PayPal in 1999, which was acquired by eBay in 2002 for $1.5 billion. Musk also founded SpaceX in 2002, an aerospace manufacturer and space transportation company. Martin Eberhard was pushed out of Tesla in 2007 and later sued Musk over the title of co-founder. Tesla's major products include the Model S, Model 3, Model X, Model Y, Cybertruck, and Solar Roof. The company also manufactures battery energy storage systems through Tesla Energy.
