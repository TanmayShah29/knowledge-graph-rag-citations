Meta Platforms, Inc., commonly known as Meta and formerly named Facebook, Inc., is an American multinational technology conglomerate headquartered in Menlo Park, California. It was founded by Mark Zuckerberg along with fellow Harvard College students and roommates Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes in 2004. The company originally launched as Facebook, a social networking website. Mark Zuckerberg was a student at Harvard when he created Facebook in his dormitory room. The platform initially limited to Harvard students but gradually expanded to other universities and then to the general public. In October 2021, Facebook, Inc. rebranded as Meta Platforms to reflect its focus on building the metaverse. Meta's products include Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, and the Meta Quest line of virtual reality headsets. Sheryl Sandberg served as Chief Operating Officer of Meta from 2008 to 2022, having previously worked at Google and the U.S. Treasury Department. Andrew Bosworth is the current CTO of Meta, leading the company's hardware and metaverse efforts.
