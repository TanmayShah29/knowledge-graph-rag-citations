Amazon.com, Inc. is an American multinational technology company focusing on e-commerce, cloud computing, online advertising, digital streaming, and artificial intelligence. It was founded by Jeff Bezos on July 5, 1994, in his garage in Bellevue, Washington. Bezos left his position as vice president of the hedge fund D.E. Shaw to start the company. Amazon originally started as an online marketplace for books but has since expanded to sell a wide variety of consumer products, digital content, and cloud computing services through Amazon Web Services (AWS). Andy Jassy succeeded Jeff Bezos as CEO on July 5, 2021. Before becoming CEO, Jassy was the founding CEO of AWS, which launched in 2006 and has become the world's most comprehensive cloud computing platform. AWS provides services to millions of customers including startups, enterprises, and government agencies. Amazon acquired Whole Foods Market in 2017 for $13.7 billion and MGM Holdings in 2022 for $8.5 billion. Jeff Bezos also owns The Washington Post newspaper, which he purchased in 2013.
